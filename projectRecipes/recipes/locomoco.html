Loco Moco

A classic dish that is native to Hawaii. It consists of 3 layers: rice, hamburger patty, gravy, and egg which allows for a savory taste.

Ingredients

  • ground beef
  • eggs
  • unsalted butter
  • sweet onion
  • green onion
  • rice
  • worcestershire sauce
  • shoyu sauce
  • ketchup
  • garlic powder
  • salt/pepper
  • cornstarch

Steps

  • Hamburger Patties
    1. Mix together ground beef with Worcestershire sauce, salt, pepper, and garlic powder in a large bowl.
    2. Start forming it into patties and rolling into a ball. Press down to about 1 inch thick.
    3. Head pan over medium heat and fry each side of the patties for about 3-5 minutes or until medium rare.
    4. Set patty aside to allow it to finish cooking.
  • Gravy
    1. Mix together the beef stock, shoyu, Worcestershire sauce, ketchup, and cornstarch. Whish until cornstarch comes off of the bottom.
    2. Using the same pan, melt 1 tbsp of butter to caramelize the onions for about a minute.
    3. Melt the rest of the butter and add in the gravy mix to the pan. Keep stirring the gravy until it begins to thicken and simmer.
  • Egg
    1. Melt a slice of butter and crack an egg onto the frying pan.
    2. Cook a sunny side up egg.
  • Assemble Loco Moco
    1. Scoop hot white rice onto a plate. Layer it with a hamburger patty then drizzle the gravy. Top with egg and garnish with green onions.
    2. Enjoy!
